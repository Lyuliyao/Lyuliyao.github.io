Biography [CV]

I will join School of Artificial Intelligence and Data Science in University of Science and Technology of China starting from Fall 2026.

I am a Hedrick Assistant Adjunct Professor in the UCLA Department of Mathematics (since Fall 2025).

I began my Ph.D. in Computational Mathematics, Science, and Engineering at Michigan State University in Fall 2020, advised by Prof. Huan Lei.

I earned my B.S. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Soochow University in July 2020, where I worked on computational chemistry and machine learning with Prof. Jingrun Chen.

I also participated in the UC Berkeley Extension Program (Spring 2019), completed a research internship at HKUST with Prof. Yang Xiang (Summer 2019), and visited UMass Dartmouth to work on discontinuous Galerkin methods with Prof. Zheng Chen (Fall 2019).

Research Interests

Machine-learning methods for multiscale modeling (model reduction)

Many-body non-Markovian dissipative particle dynamics; state-dependent general Langevin dynamics.

Algorithms for scientific machine learning

Mixed residual methods; deep-learning-based discontinuous Galerkin methods.

Gradient-free optimization

Consensus-based optimization; consensus-based sampling.
