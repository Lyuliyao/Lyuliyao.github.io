Biography [CV]

I will join UCLA Department of Mathematics as a Hedrick Assistant Adjunct Professor starting from 2025 Fall. In fall 2020, I began my Ph.D. studies in Computational Mathematics, Science, and Engineering at Michigan State University, under the supervise of Prof. Huan Lei. My journey in this field started with a bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Soochow University, which I completed in July 2020. During my undergraduate studies, I focused on Computational Chemistry and Machine Learning under the guidance of Professor Jingrun Chen. In spring 2019, I participated in the UC Berkeley Extension Program, enhancing my knowledge and skills. The following summer, I joined a research internship at HKUST, working with Professor Yang Xiang, which allowed me to delve deeper into my research interests. Additionally, in fall 2019, I had the opportunity to work on the Discontinuous Galerkin Method as a visiting student at the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth, under Professor Zheng Chen.
