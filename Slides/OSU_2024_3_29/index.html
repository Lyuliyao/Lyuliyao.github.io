Free energy constuction

  • \(\bq\): positions of the atoms in a protein
  • \(V(\bq)\): potential of the micro-scale coordinates \(\br\)
  • \(\bQ = \phi(\bq)\): CVs
  • \(U(\bQ)\): free energy surface (FES)

\[ \begin{aligned} &U (\bQ) = -\frac{1}{\beta}\ln \rho(\bQ) \\ &\rho(\bQ) = \frac{\int e^{(-\beta V(\bq))} \delta(\bs(\bq)-\bQ) \intd \br}{Z}\\ \end{aligned} \]

Constructing free energy surface is an important problem for many chemical and biology problems. It contains many important information of the complex system. For example, the minimum and the local minima usually represents the stable and metastable state of the system. FES is also important to some kinetic properties such as transition path or energy barrier between two metastable state. Usually, reconstructing FES is based on the Boltzmann relation. It relates the FES with the PDF of finding a particle in a particular state. The accurate construction of FES remains a practical challenge due to the high dimensionality and the prevalence of energy barriers.

Recent Attempts: Reinforce Dynamics (Uncertainty Indicator+ Multi DNN)

The prevalence of energy barriers means the transtion between two metastable states is a rare event.

The probability of the trasition happens will decrease exponentially with the height of the energy bar.

Several ingenious methods based on importance sampling have been developed, such as umbrella sampling, histogram reweighting,metadynamics, variational enhanced sampling

They typically add a bias potential here to increase the probability of transition from one state to the other.

The finial reconstruction of the FES is based on the PDF estimation under the bias potential

Another type of method is based on increasing the temperature artificially, the increased the temperature of decrease the energy barrier and increase the probability of transtion happens.

Main Idea

Reformulation the direct construction of \(U(\bQ)\) by seeking NN representation \( A_{\mathcal N}(\bQ)\) \(\min_{\mathcal{N}}\max_f \int_\Gamma \underbrace{\|-\nabla_\bz A_{\mathcal N}(\bQ) - \bF(\bQ)\|^2}_{\MLMN(\bQ): \text{Residual Error} } \quad f(\bQ) \intd\bQ\)
\( \bF(\bQ) \):Force Calculated from full MD
\(f(\bQ)\): Sampling Distribution

  • Minization problem:
    • optimize the NN approximation \( A_{\mathcal N}(\bQ)\) given sample points
  • Maximum problem :
    • optimize the sampling distribution \(f(\bQ)\) given the trained FES
  • Iteratively solving Minization and Maximation problems gives the constructed FES.

To achieve this goal, we introduce a min-max problem.

The min problem is common, give some sampling points in the training set, say they satisfies the distribution q

we ask minize the loss funtion defined as a L2 error of the negative gradient of parameterized FES and force sampled from full dynamics.

In max problem, give a trained FES, we want to obtain refine our training set or sampling points

Intuitively, we want to add more points where error is large, thus we maximize the loss with respect the distribution q

Maximum Problem : exploitation

Main idea: To approximate \(f^*(\bQ)\) near the max-residual point, exploit the Laplace's principle \[ \lim_{k_l\to \infty} -\frac{1}{\kappa_l} \log\left(\int \exp\left(-\kappa_l h(\bQ)\right)\intd \rho ^*(\bQ)\right) = h(\bQ^*) \] Given a collection of sample points \(\{\bQ^i\}_{i=1}^{N_w}\), construct the local quadratic approximation \[ \MLMN^-(\bQ) \approx G(\bQ) = \frac{1}{2} (\bQ-\mbm)^T V^{-1}(\bQ-\mbm) \\ f^*(\bQ)\approx \exp(-\kappa_h G(\bQ))/\tilde{Z} \] The first two moments determined by low temperature approximation \[ \mbm \approx \sum_{i=1}^{N_w} \mb Q^i \hat{p}(\mb Q^i) \quad V \approx \kappa_t \sum_{i=1}^{N_w} (\bQ^i-\mbm_t) (\bQ^i-\mbm_t)^T \hat{p}(\mb Q^i)\\ \hat{p} (\mb z) = \frac{\exp {(-\kappa_l \MLMN^-(\bz^i))}}{\sum_{i=1}^{N_w}\exp {(-\kappa_l \MLMN^-(\bz^i)})}\\ \]

The Max problem: consensus sampling

Exploitation and Exploration in McKean stochastic differential equation \[ \dot{\bQ}^i_t = - \frac{1}{\gamma}\nabla_\bQ G(\bQ^i_t;\mb m_t ,V_t) + \sqrt{\frac{2}{\kappa_h\gamma}} \mathbf\xi_i(t) \] Unlike Lagevin Equation, it is non-local and \(G\) dependes on the distribution of \(\{\bQ_t^i\}_{i=1}^{N_w}\)

Proposition:

Suppose \(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\)takes a local approximation in the quadratic form, by choosing the following first two moments with \(\kappa_t=\kappa_l+\kappa_h\) \[\mbm_t = \sum_{i=1}^{N_w} \mb Q^i \hat{p}(\mb Q^i) \quad \text{and} \quad V_t = \kappa_t \sum_{i=1}^{N_w} (\bQ^i-\mbm_t) (\bQ^i-\mbm_t)^T \hat{p}(\mb Q^i)\] The distribution of the random walkers approaches \( q_t \to \frac{\exp{(-\kappa_h \mathcal{L}^- (\mathbf{z}))}}{\int \exp {(-\kappa_h \mathcal{L}^- (\mathbf{z}))} \, d\mathbf{z}} \) as \( t \to \infty \).

Instead we treat the sampler z as a random walker z^i_t, governed by the McKean stochastic differential equation.

The adaptively constructed conservative potential function \( G(\bz_t) \) guides individual particles towards \( \bm_t \), indicative of the region of large residual error.

Main Idea:
seek a set of generalized coordinates to encode the many-body information.
\( \hat{ \mathbf Q}^k_i = \mathbf{Q}_i \oplus \sum_{l\in\mathcal{N}_i} f^k(|\mathbf{Q}_{il}|) \mathbf{Q}_{il} \)
\( \hat{ \bQ}_{ij} = [\hat{\mathbf{Q}}_{ij}^{1},\cdot,\hat{\mathbf{Q}}_{ij}^{K}], \quad \hat{\mathbf{Q}}_{ij}^{k} = \hat{\mathbf{Q}}_i^{k} - \hat{\mathbf{Q}}_j^{k} \)
\(\{f^k\}_{k=1}^K\) encodes the local environment of individual CG particles
\(\hat{ \bQ}_{i}^k \) preserves the translational and rotational symmetry and the permutation invariance.

Symmetry-preserving representation

Symmetry preserving form of the memory term \(\mathbf{K}(\bQ,t)\)
\(\mathbf{K}(\bQ,t) = \mathbf{\Xi}(\bQ) \exp(-\Lambda t) \mathbf{\Xi}(\bQ) \)
\(\mathbf{\Xi}_{ij} = [\mathbf{\Xi}_{ij}^1,\cdots \mathbf{\Xi}_{ij}^n] \quad n\) number of features \(\boldsymbol{\Xi}^m_{ij} = \sum_{k} h_{m,k}(\hat{ \mathbf Q}_{ij}^T \hat{ \mathbf Q}_{ij} ) \hat{ \mathbf Q}_{ij}^k \otimes \hat{ \mathbf Q}_{ij}^k\)
\(\boldsymbol{\Xi}^m_{ii} = \sum_{j\neq i} \mathbf{\Xi}^m_{ij} \)
\(h\) map the local environment onto the friction matrix \(\boldsymbol{\Xi}^m_{ij}\)

Proposition: The above form of \(\mathbf{K}(\bQ,t)\) strictly preserves the translational, rotational and permutational symmetry constraints
