\begin{abstract}
    We propose Multiscale Nudge, a cross-scale data assimilation framework for injecting macroscopic observations into microscopic mean-field dynamics. By formulating the observation mismatch as a quadratic functional on probability measures, we design a Wasserstein-gradient-based nudging mechanism whose particle realization is computationally straightforward, avoiding both model linearization and ensemble-level covariance management. Under regularity assumptions and exact observations, we prove exponential decay of the $L^2$ error up to a bias floor determined by model misspecification. Numerical experiments on linear, bimodal, chaotic, kinetic, and collective-motion systems show that the proposed method consistently improves forecast accuracy and recovers macroscopic structure from incomplete observations.
\end{abstract}
