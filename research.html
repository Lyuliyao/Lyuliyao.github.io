Machine Learning and Multiscale Modeling

Developing learning methodologies that enhance bottom‑up multiscale modeling and accurately predict complex physical phenomena.

Non‑conservative Memory Terms for CGMD

  1. Many‑body dissipative interactions. ML‑based coarse‑grained MD using Mori–Zwanzig to include non‑Markovian many‑body memory.
  2. Symmetry‑aware neural networks. Networks respect rotational, translational, and permutation symmetries to model friction matrices.
  3. Beyond mean‑field metrics. Built on Mori–Zwanzig, inheriting heterogeneous information rather than only matching VACF.

Coarse‑Grained Models for Non‑Equilibrium Processes

  1. Generalization challenge. Non‑equilibrium applicability is limited by inconsistent conditional distributions.
  2. Training CG variables. Time‑lagged ICA to find auxiliary CG variables minimizing the entropy contribution of unresolved modes.

Algorithms of Scientific Machine Learning

Deep Mixed Residual Method (MIM) for high‑order PDEs

  1. Mixed residual. Rewrite a high‑order PDE as a first‑order system (akin to LDG and mixed FEM).
  2. Flexible networks. Share or separate DNNs for different unknowns as needed.
  3. Built‑in boundary conditions. Construct DNNs that satisfy boundary/initial conditions by design.

Deep Learning‑based Discontinuous Galerkin (D2GM)

  1. Handles discontinuities. Weak formulation with discontinuous bases approximates shocks accurately.
  2. High‑dimensional uncertainty. Mini‑batch sampling controls sample count and mitigates curse of dimensionality.

Gradient‑Free Optimization Methods

Exploring optimization strategies that bypass gradient calculations for complex, multi‑modal problems (e.g., consensus‑based optimization/sampling, finite‑horizon stochastic control).
